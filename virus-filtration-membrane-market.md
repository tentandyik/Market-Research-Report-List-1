<p><p>The virus filtration membrane market features key types including ultrafiltration and nanofiltration membranes. Ultrafiltration membranes utilize a porous structure to separate larger viruses and contaminants, effectively allowing smaller molecules and solvents to pass through. Nanofiltration membranes, on the other hand, have finer pores that provide enhanced selectivity, enabling the removal of smaller viral particles while retaining essential solutes. Both types are crucial in applications such as water purification, biotechnology, and pharmaceuticals, ensuring safety and quality in various processes.</p></p>
